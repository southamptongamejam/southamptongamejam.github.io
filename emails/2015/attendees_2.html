Southampton Game Jam

January 23 - 25, 2015

Building 59, Highfield Campus, University Road, Southampton SO15 1BJ
 

Hello jammers!

The Global Game Jam is coming up this Friday (23rd), and you have tickets! This email is to remind you to attend and also covers some useful information about the event.

If you are unable attend, please cancel your ticket (or reply to this e-mail), so those on the waiting list have a chance to attend.

= Location =

The whole event will be on the University of Southampton’s Highfield Campus:
3pm - 6pm: Registration and keynotes in Building 54 ("School of Mathematics")
6pm onwards: group forming, pizza and game making in Building 59 (“Zepler”)
Please try and turn up in time for the start of the event. If, for any reason, you arrive later make sure you speak to an organiser!

This map may help: http://maps.southampton.ac.uk/#1/18/50.93692/-1.39749

Both Building 54 (3pm-6pm: intro talks) and Zepler (B59 - 6pm onwards) are less than 5 minute’s walk from the Highfield bus interchange and the car parks.

= Parking =

Before 5pm, you will either need to park in the Pay and Display by Building 16 (north east of campus, on the corner of Burgess Road and University Road), or on local roads (which mostly have 2 hour parking).

You can park on Highfield campus for free from 5pm. Upper Nuffield car park, just off Salisbury Road is where you want!

= Photography =

We will be taking photos and videos at the event. These may be used for promotional purposes. If you DO NOT want to be photographed or recorded, you should email us back at this address.

= GGJ website =

We’ve had a few questions about this. We will enable the ability to sign up to the Southampton site on the Global Game Jam website on Friday. This is to ensure everyone attending signs up through Eventbrite.

= Food and Drink =

We’re pleased to announce we’ll be providing food Friday night, Saturday lunchtime and Saturday evening, as well as snacks during the closing event on Sunday afternoon.

This means you’ll have to arrange your own breakfast on Saturday and Sunday, and lunch on Sunday, as well as drinks and snacks in between. You may want to bring something, but there are also many shops, take-aways and restaurants within walking distance of the site.

= Sponsors =

We would like to thank our sponsors, without whom this game jam wouldn't be as good as it's going to be! We’re proud to be sponsored by Boss Alien and Natural Motion Games, as well as the University of Southampton, the British Computer Society, the Southampton Code Dojo and the Southampton Maker Space.

= More questions =

Check the FAQ on our website. If you’ve got any questions we’ve not answered here, send us an email at: organiser@southamptongamejam.org

Not long now! We’re really looking forward to seeing you all at the event, and seeing the great games you are going to create!

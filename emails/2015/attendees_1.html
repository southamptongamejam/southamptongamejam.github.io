Southampton Game Jam

January 23 - 25, 2015

Building 59, Highfield Campus, University Road, Southampton SO15 1BJ
 

Hello jammers!

The Global Game Jam is coming up on Friday 23rd January, and you have tickets! This email is to remind you to attend and also covers some useful information about the event.

If you are unable attend, please cancel your ticket (or reply to this e-mail), so those on the waiting list have a chance to attend.

= Friday Schedule =
  • Registration from 3pm on Friday.
  • Keynote and theme announcement starts at 5pm on Friday.

Please try and turn up in time for the start of the event. If, for any reason, you can’t make it then don’t worry, you can still take part, but make sure you speak to an organiser as soon as you arrive!

= Preparation =

Before you arrive, we advise that you:

  • set up a Global Game Jam account.
  • plan your version control (or learn to use one) - we recommend Github for code and Dropbox for other assets, but use what you’re comfortable with.
  • install any other specialist software you need on your laptop. set up eduroam wireless on your laptop, if you’re from a university.

You may also want to book a nearby hotel (or consider heading home) to get a good night’s sleep on Friday: this will help you make the best game that you can on Saturday and Sunday!

= What to bring =
  • Please bring your own laptop if you have one. Don’t forget your charger! We have wireless and lots of 4-gang power strip extension leads.
  • If you are staying over, bring a sleeping bag, pillow and towel.
= What we will provide =
Equipment
  • Wireless.
  • 4-gang power strips (please don’t bring your own).
  • Some machines with Unity, Blender, Audacity and various other development tools.
  • Dice, tokens, card and Sharpies for boardgames and paper prototyping.
Facilities
  • Toilets
  • Showers
  • A floor to sleep on (no beds!) - over 18s only
Food
  • Pizza Friday night.
  • Lunch on Saturday.
  • Dinner Saturday night.
  • Snacks and wine at the closing on Sunday.

The site is open for the whole 48 hours, although there is no obligation to stay throughout: you can come and go as you please.

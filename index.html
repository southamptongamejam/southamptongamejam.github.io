About

Southampton Game Jam is a local site for the Global Game Jam 2015, which will take part on the weekend of 23rd-25th January 2015. Our site will be at the University of Southampton Highfield Campus and will be open for the whole 48 hours.

We are currently looking for participants, volunteer co-ordinators and sponsors for the event.

Location

Getting here by bus

All the Unilink buses pass through the Highfield Campus interchange. From the town centre, take a U1A, U1W or U2B.

Getting here by coach

Many National Express services to Southampton stop at the Highfield Campus interchange.

Getting here by car

From the north, follow the M3 until you see signs for the A33. The motorway forks, but you can take either side for the A33, which is a slip lane just after the fork. Go straight ahead at 2 roundabouts, and when you reach the major, traffic lit junction with Burgess Road, turn left, following signposts for Fareham and University.

Getting here by train

Highfield Campus is a 25 minute bus journey, or 10 minute taxi ride, from both Southampton Airport Parkway and Southampton Central, so get a train to whichever is most convenient. Trains from London Waterloo take around 1 hour 20 minutes. Trains from Brighton, a little under 2 hours. Bournemouth is about 45 minutes away, and the train from Portsmouth takes an hour.

From Southampton Airport Parkway, take the U1C bus to Highfield campus.

From Southampton Central, take the U1A, U1W, U1E or U2B bus to Highfield campus.

FAQ

1. Do I need to form a team before I turn up?

No. We expect most people will come along without having already formed a team. There will be time available to form teams and it's a great way to meet new people.

2. Where can I get food when at the event?

We're currently figuring out whether we can provide any food. Check back soon for an update.

There are many convenience shops and takeaways within 5-10 minutes walk, as well as some food outlets in the Students Union on campus.

3. What should I bring?

Anything you might need to make games! A laptop will probably help. Your charger is essential :-) If you do audio or art, some of the tools for that might help you do things quicker.

Please don't bring 4-gangs/multiway adapter plugs/extension leads. We will provide ones that have been H&S tested.

A change of clothes, a towel, shower gel, a toothbrush, a sleeping bag and a pillow will help if you're planning to stay on-site.

You may also want to bring some cash for buying food.

4. Are there any useful resources I should be aware of?

Take a look at the list on the Global Game Jam site.

5. Can I stay over for the full 48 hours?

Yes, if you're 18 years old or older. We will not be providing beds. There will be a quiet room in the building that will be designated for sleeping/rest. You should bring a sleeping bag and pillow if you wish to make use of this room.

You can of course stay in a hotel if you'd prefer, but it is up to you to book and pay for it.

We advise considering staying in a hotel for the Friday night, to let your game ideas brew and wake up fresh and ready to make during Saturday and Sunday!

6. Is there a shower in the building?

Yes, there is a shower you may use, but you need to bring your own towel and soap/shower gel.
